Policy Impact Timeline & Analysis

Critical policy changes and their quantified impact on Indian student migration patterns

1
January 2024
Canada Introduces International Student Caps
Canada implemented enrollment caps for international students, limiting growth and creating uncertainty in the market. This policy directly targeted volume growth that had occurred in previous years.
Impact: 41% decline in Indian student enrollment
2
March 2024
UK Graduate Route Under Review
The UK government announced a review of the Graduate Route visa, creating uncertainty about post-study work opportunities. This caused a temporary decline in applications before the route was eventually retained.
Impact: 28% temporary decline, later recovered to +11%
3
August 2024
USA Streamlines Visa Processing
The United States implemented significant improvements to visa processing times and procedures, reducing wait times and increasing approval rates for Indian students, particularly in STEM fields.
Impact: 23% growth, regaining market leadership
4
November 2024
Germany Enhances Post-Study Work Options
Germany introduced enhanced post-study work opportunities and streamlined permanent residency pathways for international graduates, making it more attractive for long-term career planning.
Impact: 67% growth, emerging as preferred destination
5
February 2025
Australia Maintains Stable Policies
Australia continued its consistent approach to international education policy, maintaining stable visa processing and post-study work rights, leading to predictable growth patterns.
Impact: Steady 15% growth through policy consistency
